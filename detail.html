With Dodgers, Dave Roberts Has Another Chance to Haunt the Yankees

45
Billy Witz, 12 Sept 2016, 01:29

Roberts, whose pivotal steal started the Red Sox’ rally over the Yankees in the 2004 A.L.C.S., visits the Bronx this week as manager of the Dodgers.

The finer points of gaining a proper lead and studying a pitcher’s delivery could help him steal a base. But what Wills, the former Los Angeles Dodgers player who transformed baseball in the 1960s with his base running, wanted to prepare Roberts for was something bigger — stealing a moment.

“I said, ‘Dave, one day you’re going to be on first base and get the steal sign and there’s not going to be the element of surprise,’” Wills said recently by phone from his home in Sedona, Ariz. “Everybody in the ballpark knows you’re going to run, the whole country is going to be watching on TV, and that pitcher is going to be on his best with pickoffs, but you’ve still got to go.”

Wills added, “That is when you’re a real base stealer.”

At the time, it seemed an extraordinary show of faith.

Roberts, then 30, barely had had 150 at-bats in the major leagues. The Dodgers had acquired him from Cleveland before the 2002 season for two minor leaguers, but there were few expectations he would make the team, let alone an impact.

Still, Roberts parlayed a strong spring into a spot as the Dodgers’ opening-day center fielder and leadoff hitter.
